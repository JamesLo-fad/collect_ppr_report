### 1.2 Key Success Patterns Identified

#### A. Research Design Characteristics

**Quantitative Studies (Most Common)**
- Sample sizes typically range from 1,500-2,500 respondents
- Use of probability sampling (simple random sampling, stratified sampling)
- Response rates reported: 45-60% considered acceptable
- Statistical methods: Regression analysis, SEM, CFA, HLM
- Clear operationalization of variables with validated instruments

**Qualitative Studies**
- In-depth interviews: 6-30 participants typical
- Focus groups: 4-10 groups with 3-7 participants each
- Purposive and convenience sampling acknowledged
- Thematic analysis or grounded theory approaches

**Mixed-Methods Studies (Highly Valued)**
- Triangulation of quantitative surveys + qualitative interviews
- Sequential or concurrent designs
- Integration of findings from multiple data sources
- Example: Survey (1,500+) + Focus Groups (40 participants) + In-depth Interviews (20-30)

#### B. Budget Ranges by Project Type

| Project Type | Budget Range (HKD) | Duration |
|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| Small-scale qualitative | 300,000-500,000 | 9-12 months |
| Medium mixed-methods | 500,000-800,000 | 12-18 months |
| Large-scale quantitative | 800,000-1,000,000 | 15-24 months |

#### C. Common Theoretical Frameworks Used

1. **Policy Analysis Frameworks**
   - Agenda-Setting Theory (McCombs & Shaw)
   - Policy Implementation Theory
   - Public Choice Theory

2. **Social Science Frameworks**
   - Social Capital Theory
   - Welfare Deservingness (CARIN Framework)
   - Stakeholder Analysis

3. **Evaluation Frameworks**
   - SWOT Analysis
   - Cost-Benefit Analysis
   - Outcome Simulation Models

### 1.3 Structural Elements of Successful Reports

#### Standard Report Structure (Observed Pattern)

1. **Cover Page** - Standardized PPR format with bilingual text
2. **Research Team** - PI + Co-Is with institutional affiliations
3. **Abstract** (English + Chinese) - 300-500 words each
4. **Executive Summary** - 2-4 pages with key findings
5. **Layman Summary on Policy Implications** - Required section
6. **Main Body**:
   - Background/Introduction
   - Literature Review
   - Research Objectives
   - Methodology
   - Results/Findings
   - Discussion
   - Policy Implications & Recommendations
7. **References**
8. **Appendices** (questionnaires, interview guides, etc.)

---

### 1.4 Writing Style & Tone Analysis

#### Language Characteristics
- **Formal academic tone** throughout
- **Bilingual presentation** (English + Chinese) for key sections
- **Third-person perspective** in methodology and findings
- **Active voice** for recommendations
- **Precise quantitative language** ("statistically significant," "p < 0.05")

#### Effective Phrases Observed
- "This study aims to examine..."
- "The findings reveal that..."
- "Policy implications include..."
- "It is recommended that the government..."
- "Evidence suggests..."
- "The results indicate..."

---

### 1.5 Policy Relevance Strategies

#### How Successful Projects Demonstrate Policy Relevance

1. **Direct Government Policy Connection**
   - Reference to specific Policy Address initiatives
   - Alignment with government strategic priorities
   - Citation of relevant ordinances and regulations

2. **Stakeholder Engagement**
   - Government departments consulted
   - NGO partnerships established
   - Community organizations involved

3. **Actionable Recommendations**
   - Specific, implementable policy suggestions
   - Cost-benefit considerations included
   - Phased implementation approaches

4. **Evidence-Based Approach**
   - Local data collection (Hong Kong-specific)
   - International comparisons where relevant
   - Longitudinal or comparative designs

---

### 1.6 Common Methodological Approaches

#### Quantitative Methods
- **Telephone surveys** (CATI) - most common for general population
- **Face-to-face interviews** - for specific populations
- **Online surveys** - increasingly used
- **Secondary data analysis** - government statistics, registries

#### Qualitative Methods
- **Semi-structured interviews** - most common
- **Focus group discussions** - 6-10 groups typical
- **Case studies** - for policy implementation analysis
- **Document analysis** - policy documents, legislation

#### Analytical Techniques
- Regression analysis (multiple, logistic)
- Structural Equation Modeling (SEM)
- Confirmatory Factor Analysis (CFA)
- Thematic analysis
- Content analysis
- SWOT analysis

```

Despite these efforts, [gap or challenge] remains. Previous research has shown

this study will contribute to [broader policy goals].
```
